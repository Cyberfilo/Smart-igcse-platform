Rate = amount per unit. Same formula pattern for speed, density, pressure.

Common rates
Speed\(\text{speed} = \dfrac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}}\)
Density\(\rho = \dfrac{\text{mass}}{\text{volume}}\)
Pressure\(P = \dfrac{F}{A}\)
Flow\(\text{rate} = \dfrac{\text{quantity}}{\text{time}}\)
Average vs instantaneous
Average speed \(= \dfrac{\text{total distance}}{\text{total time}}\) (NOT average of individual speeds)
Example Travel \(30\text{ km}\) at \(60\text{ km/h}\), then \(30\text{ km}\) at \(30\text{ km/h}\). Total time \(= 0.5 + 1 = 1.5\text{ h}\). Average speed \(= \tfrac{60}{1.5} = 40\text{ km/h}\).
Unit conversion km/h → m/s: multiply by \(\dfrac{1000}{3600} = \dfrac{5}{18}\). m/s → km/h: multiply by \(\dfrac{18}{5}\). Always check units match before combining rates.
