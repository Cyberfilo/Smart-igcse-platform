(Extended.) Differentiating gives the gradient function \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\). Evaluate at a specific \(x\) to get the tangent slope there.

Power rule\(y = ax^{n} \Rightarrow \dfrac{dy}{dx} = an\,x^{n-1}\)
  • Constants differentiate to \(0\)
  • Polynomials: differentiate term by term
  • Gradient at \(x = a\): substitute \(a\) into \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\)
  • Stationary points: solve \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 0\)
Second derivative test
\(\dfrac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}} > 0\) → minimum
\(\dfrac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}} < 0\) → maximum
Tangent at \((x_{1}, y_{1})\)\(y - y_{1} = m(x - x_{1})\), \(m = \dfrac{dy}{dx}\)
Normal at \((x_{1}, y_{1})\)same form, gradient \(= -\dfrac{1}{m}\)
Example \(y = 2x^{3} - 4x^{2} + 5x - 7\) → \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 6x^{2} - 8x + 5\). At \(x = 2\): gradient \(= 24 - 16 + 5 = 13\).
