(Extended.) Differentiating gives the gradient function dy/dx. Evaluate at a specific x to get the tangent slope there.

Power ruley = ax^n → dy/dx = an·x^(n−1)
  • Constants differentiate to 0
  • Polynomials: differentiate term by term
  • Gradient at x = a: substitute a into dy/dx
  • Stationary points: solve dy/dx = 0
Second derivative test
d²y/dx² > 0 → minimum
d²y/dx² < 0 → maximum
Tangent at (x₁, y₁)y − y₁ = m(x − x₁), m = dy/dx
Normal at (x₁, y₁)same form, gradient = −1/m
Example y = 2x³ − 4x² + 5x − 7 → dy/dx = 6x² − 8x + 5. At x = 2: gradient = 24 − 16 + 5 = 13.
