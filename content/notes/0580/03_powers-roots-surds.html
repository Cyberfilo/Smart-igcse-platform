Powers are repeated multiplication; roots undo them. Surds keep exact irrational values.

Core powers & roots
Square\(a^{2} = a \times a\)
Square root\(\sqrt{a^{2}} = a\) (\(a \ge 0\))
Cube\(a^{3} = a \times a \times a\)
Cube root\(\sqrt[3]{a^{3}} = a\)
Surd rules (Extended)
\(\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{ab}\)  |  \(\sqrt{a} \div \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a/b}\)  |  \((a + \sqrt{b})(a - \sqrt{b}) = a^{2} - b\)
Simplify \(\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = 6\sqrt{2}\) · \(\sqrt{18} + \sqrt{50} = 3\sqrt{2} + 5\sqrt{2} = 8\sqrt{2}\)
Rationalise \(\dfrac{3}{\sqrt{5}} = \dfrac{3\sqrt{5}}{5}\) · \(\dfrac{1}{2 + \sqrt{3}} = \dfrac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{4 - 3} = 2 - \sqrt{3}\)
Watch out \(\sqrt{16} = 4\) is rational. Only \(\sqrt{n}\) where \(n\) isn't a perfect square is irrational.
