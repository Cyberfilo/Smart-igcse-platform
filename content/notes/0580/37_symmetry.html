Line symmetry = mirror. Rotational symmetry = carousel — how many times does it look the same in one full turn?

2D symmetry
  • Equilateral △: 3 lines of symmetry, order 3 rotational
  • Square: 4 lines, order 4
  • Rectangle: 2 lines, order 2
  • Rhombus: 2 lines (along diagonals), order 2
  • Regular hexagon: 6 lines, order 6
  • Circle: infinite lines, infinite order
3D symmetry (Extended)
  • Planes of symmetry (2D extended): cube has 9, cuboid (distinct sides) has 3
  • Axes of rotational symmetry: cube has 13 axes total
  • Cylinder: ∞ rotational about central axis
Example For a regular pentagon, lines of symmetry = 5. Rotational order = 5 (looks identical after 72° rotation).
Order 1 "Order of rotational symmetry = 1" means NO rotational symmetry — only returns to itself after a full 360° turn. Sometimes phrased as "no rotational symmetry".
