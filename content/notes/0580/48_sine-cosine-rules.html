(Extended.) Non-right triangles need sine or cosine rule. Pick based on what you're given.

Sine rule
\(\dfrac{a}{\sin A} = \dfrac{b}{\sin B} = \dfrac{c}{\sin C}\)
Use whenYou know an angle and its opposite side, plus one more angle or side.
Cosine rule
\(a^{2} = b^{2} + c^{2} - 2bc\cos A\)
Rearranged\(\cos A = \dfrac{b^{2} + c^{2} - a^{2}}{2bc}\)
Use whenYou know all three sides, OR two sides and the included angle.
Area of a triangle
Area \(= \tfrac{1}{2}\,a\,b\,\sin C\) — two sides and the included angle
Example (cosine rule) \(a = 7\), \(b = 5\), \(C = 60°\). \(c^{2} = 49 + 25 - 70\cos 60° = 74 - 35 = 39\). \(c = \sqrt{39} \approx 6.24\).
Ambiguous case Sine rule can give two valid angles when solving for an angle (\(\theta\) or \(180 - \theta\)). Sketch and check which makes sense.
