(Extended.) Running total of frequencies. The curve lets you read off median, quartiles, and IQR.

Building the table
  • Add cumulative column: running total of frequency
  • Plot at the UPPER boundary of each class (not midpoint)
  • Join with a smooth curve (or straight line) through all points
Reading the curve
Medianat cumulative \(= \tfrac{n}{2}\)
Lower quartile (\(Q_{1}\))at cumulative \(= \tfrac{n}{4}\)
Upper quartile (\(Q_{3}\))at cumulative \(= \tfrac{3n}{4}\)
IQR\(Q_{3} - Q_{1}\)
Example \(60\) students. Median at cumulative \(30\) → read across to find corresponding value. \(Q_{1}\) at \(15\), \(Q_{3}\) at \(45\). IQR \(= Q_{3} - Q_{1}\).
"Less than" count To find "how many scored less than \(X\)", read UP from \(X\) on the horizontal axis to the curve. Cumulative freq at that point \(=\) count.
