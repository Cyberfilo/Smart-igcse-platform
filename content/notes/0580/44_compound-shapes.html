Split complex figures into shapes you know, compute each piece, then add or subtract.

Strategy
  • Addition: L-shape = two rectangles · semicircle on square
  • Subtraction: shaded region = outer − inner · ring = big circle − small circle
  • Label every dimension you derive — don't reuse unknown variables across pieces
Common compound solids
  • Cylinder + hemisphere = ice-cream cone: V = πr²h + ⅔πr³
  • Cube with cylinder drilled out: V = s³ − πr²s
  • Frustum (cone with top cut): V = ⅓πh(R² + Rr + r²)
Example L-shape: 10 × 6 rectangle with a 3 × 4 rectangle removed from one corner. Area = 60 − 12 = 48.
Shaded regions Read carefully: "shaded area = outer − inner" almost always. Sketch and label before computing.
