Prisms and cylinders: volume \(=\) cross-section area \(\times\) length. Pyramids, cones, spheres: dedicated formulas.

Volumes
Cuboid\(V = l \times w \times h\)
Prism\(V = \text{cross-section} \times \text{length}\)
Cylinder\(V = \pi r^{2} h\)
Pyramid\(V = \tfrac{1}{3} \times \text{base} \times h\)
Cone\(V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^{2} h\)
Sphere\(V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^{3}\)
Surface areas
Cylinder\(A = 2\pi r^{2} + 2\pi r h\)
Cone (curved)\(A = \pi r l\) (slant \(l\))
Sphere\(A = 4\pi r^{2}\)
Example Cylinder \(r = 3\), \(h = 10\). \(V = \pi \cdot 9 \cdot 10 = 90\pi\). Total SA \(= 2\pi(9) + 2\pi(3)(10) = 78\pi \approx 245\).
Slant length for cones \(l\) (slant) \(= \sqrt{r^{2} + h^{2}}\) by Pythagoras. Don't confuse with vertical \(h\) — only slant \(l\) appears in the curved SA formula.
