Prisms and cylinders: volume = cross-section area × length. Pyramids, cones, spheres: dedicated formulas.

Volumes
CuboidV = l × w × h
PrismV = cross-section × length
CylinderV = πr²h
PyramidV = ⅓ × base × h
ConeV = ⅓πr²h
SphereV = (4/3)πr³
Surface areas
CylinderA = 2πr² + 2πrh
Cone (curved)A = πrl (slant l)
SphereA = 4πr²
Example Cylinder r = 3, h = 10. V = π·9·10 = 90π. Total SA = 2π(9) + 2π(3)(10) = 78π ≈ 245.
Slant length for cones l (slant) = √(r² + h²) by Pythagoras. Don't confuse with vertical h — only slant l appears in the curved SA formula.
