Gradient measures steepness. Equation \(y = mx + c\) captures a line completely: \(m\) = slope, \(c\) = \(y\)-intercept.

Gradient\(m = \dfrac{y_{2} - y_{1}}{x_{2} - x_{1}}\)
Line through point\(y - y_{1} = m(x - x_{1})\)
Finding the equation
  • Given 2 points: compute \(m\), then use point-slope form
  • Given gradient + 1 point: straight into point-slope
  • Given slope + \(y\)-intercept: read off \(y = mx + c\)
Example Line through \((1, 3)\) and \((4, 9)\). \(m = \dfrac{9 - 3}{4 - 1} = 2\). \(y - 3 = 2(x - 1)\) → \(y = 2x + 1\).
Horizontal vs vertical Horizontal line: \(m = 0\), equation \(y = k\). Vertical line: \(m\) undefined, equation \(x = k\) (not in \(y = mx + c\) form).
