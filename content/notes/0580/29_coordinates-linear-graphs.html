Points are (x, y). Lines through points follow y = mx + c.

Coordinate basics
  • x before y, origin O = (0, 0)
  • 4 quadrants: (+,+), (−,+), (−,−), (+,−) anticlockwise from top-right
  • Horizontal lines: y = k · Vertical lines: x = k
Drawing a linear graph
Pick any 3 x-values, compute y, plot, join with a ruler.
Example Draw y = 2x − 1 for −2 ≤ x ≤ 3. Table: x = −2, 0, 3 → y = −5, −1, 5. Plot and join.
Always use 3 points Two points define a line, but the third catches arithmetic slips — if it's off the line, you know one of your y-values is wrong.
