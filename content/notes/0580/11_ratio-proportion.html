Ratio compares parts. Direct proportion keeps the ratio constant; inverse proportion keeps the product constant.

Sharing in a ratio
Total shares = sum of ratio terms. One share = amount ÷ total shares.
Example Split £120 in ratio 2 : 3 : 7. Total = 12 shares → 1 share = £10 → £20 : £30 : £70.
Direct & inverse proportion (Extended)
Directy ∝ x → y = kx
Inversey ∝ 1/x → y = k/x
Squarey ∝ x² → y = kx²
Square rooty ∝ √x → y = k√x
Find k first y ∝ x² and y = 18 when x = 3 → k = 18/9 = 2 → y = 2x². When x = 5: y = 50.
Reading ratios "A : B = 2 : 5" means A/(A+B) = 2/7, NOT 2/5. The fraction's denominator is the total, not the other part.
