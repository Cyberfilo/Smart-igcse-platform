Ratio compares parts. Direct proportion keeps the ratio constant; inverse proportion keeps the product constant.

Sharing in a ratio
Total shares = sum of ratio terms. One share = amount \(\div\) total shares.
Example Split £120 in ratio \(2 : 3 : 7\). Total \(= 12\) shares → 1 share \(= £10\) → \(£20 : £30 : £70\).
Direct & inverse proportion (Extended)
Direct\(y \propto x \Rightarrow y = kx\)
Inverse\(y \propto \dfrac{1}{x} \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{k}{x}\)
Square\(y \propto x^{2} \Rightarrow y = kx^{2}\)
Square root\(y \propto \sqrt{x} \Rightarrow y = k\sqrt{x}\)
Find k first \(y \propto x^{2}\) and \(y = 18\) when \(x = 3\) → \(k = \tfrac{18}{9} = 2\) → \(y = 2x^{2}\). When \(x = 5\): \(y = 50\).
Reading ratios "\(A : B = 2 : 5\)" means \(\dfrac{A}{A+B} = \dfrac{2}{7}\), NOT \(\dfrac{2}{5}\). The fraction's denominator is the total, not the other part.
