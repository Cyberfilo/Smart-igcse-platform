(Extended.) Four transformations applied to \(y = f(x)\). Changes inside the bracket are horizontal and counter-intuitive.

The four transformations
Vertical shift\(y = f(x) + a\) → up by \(a\)
Horizontal shift\(y = f(x - a)\) → right by \(a\)
Vertical stretch\(y = k\,f(x)\) → \(\times k\) in \(y\)
Horizontal stretch\(y = f(kx)\) → \(\div k\) in \(x\)
Reflections
In \(x\)-axis\(y = -f(x)\)
In \(y\)-axis\(y = f(-x)\)
Example Start from \(y = x^{2}\). Then \(y = (x - 2)^{2} + 3\) is the parabola shifted right 2, up 3. Vertex at \((2, 3)\).
Inside vs outside Inside the bracket acts on \(x\) → horizontal and opposite to intuition (\(-\) shifts right; \(\times k\) shrinks). Outside acts on \(y\) → vertical and direct.
