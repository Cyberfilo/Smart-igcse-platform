Vocabulary you must know on sight — the language of geometry questions.

Triangles
  • Equilateral: all 3 sides equal, all 3 angles 60°
  • Isosceles: 2 sides equal, base angles equal
  • Scalene: all sides and angles different
  • Right-angled: one 90° angle
Quadrilaterals
  • Square: 4 equal sides, 4 right angles
  • Rectangle: 4 right angles, opposite sides equal
  • Rhombus: 4 equal sides, diagonals perpendicular
  • Parallelogram: 2 pairs parallel sides
  • Trapezium: exactly 1 pair parallel sides
  • Kite: 2 pairs of adjacent equal sides
Polygons
Sum of interior angles(n − 2) × 180°
Each interior angle (regular)(n − 2) × 180° / n
Each exterior angle (regular)360° / n
Solids
  • Prism: constant cross-section · Pyramid: one vertex + polygonal base
  • Sphere, cylinder, cone: curved surfaces · Faces / edges / vertices: Euler: F + V − E = 2
