Standard form packs very big / very small numbers into one tidy expression.

Form\(A \times 10^{n}\) where \(1 \le A < 10\), \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\)
Converting
Big number → std formcount digits moved LEFT to \(n\)
Small number → std formcount digits moved RIGHT to \(-n\)
Example \(6\,300\,000 = 6.3 \times 10^{6}\) · \(0.00042 = 4.2 \times 10^{-4}\)
Arithmetic \((3 \times 10^{5}) \times (2 \times 10^{3}) = 6 \times 10^{8}\) · \((8 \times 10^{6}) \div (4 \times 10^{2}) = 2 \times 10^{4}\)
Adjusting A Calculator may give \(32 \times 10^{4}\) — not standard. Rewrite as \(3.2 \times 10^{5}\). The constraint \(1 \le A < 10\) is non-negotiable.
