(Extended only.) Growth/decay where the change is proportional to current size. Same algebra as compound interest with arbitrary units of time.

Growth\(N(t) = N_{0}\,k^{t}\) (\(k > 1\))
Decay\(N(t) = N_{0}\,k^{t}\) (\(0 < k < 1\))
Finding the multiplier k
\(k = \left(\dfrac{N_{\text{new}}}{N_{\text{old}}}\right)^{\!1/\Delta t}\)  |  per-% rate \(r\) → \(k = 1 \pm \dfrac{r}{100}\)
Population Bacteria double every 30 min. Start \(500\). After 2 h (4 doublings): \(N = 500 \times 2^{4} = 8000\).
Half-life Substance halves every 5 years. Start \(80\text{ g}\). After 15 years (3 halvings): \(80 \times \left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{3} = 10\text{ g}\).
Reading graphs Exponential curves never touch zero. Decay asymptotes to \(y = 0\); growth accelerates without bound.
