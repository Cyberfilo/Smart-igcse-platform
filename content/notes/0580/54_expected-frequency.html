Relative frequency estimates probability from data. Expected frequency predicts what should happen in N trials.

Relative frequency
relative frequency = successes / total trials
As an estimateP(event) ≈ relative frequency (as trials → ∞)
Expected frequency
Expected = n × P(event)
Example (relative) A biased coin lands heads 62 times out of 100 flips. Estimated P(H) = 0.62.
Example (expected) A spinner: P(red) = 0.3. In 200 spins, expected reds = 200 × 0.3 = 60.
Relative vs theoretical Relative frequency uses data; theoretical probability uses model. If they disagree strongly, suspect bias or low trial count.
