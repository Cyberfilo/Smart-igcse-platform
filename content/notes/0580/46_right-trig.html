SOH CAH TOA — three ratios in any right-angled triangle. Label the triangle first; ratio second.

\(\sin\theta\)\(\dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}\)
\(\cos\theta\)\(\dfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}\)
\(\tan\theta\)\(\dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}\)
Finding a side
Pick the ratio that links the known side \(+\) unknown side \(+\) angle. Solve for the unknown.
Finding an angle
\(\theta = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\right),\ \cos^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}\right),\ \text{or}\ \tan^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\right)\)
Example (side) Right triangle, hypotenuse \(10\), angle \(35°\). Opposite \(= 10 \times \sin 35° \approx 5.74\).
Example (angle) Adjacent \(= 8\), hypotenuse \(= 17\). \(\cos\theta = \tfrac{8}{17}\) → \(\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\tfrac{8}{17}\right) \approx 61.9°\).
Label matters Opposite and adjacent are relative to the angle you're using, not fixed. Re-label when the angle of interest changes.
