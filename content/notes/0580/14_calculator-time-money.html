Utility topics examiners still test: calculator use, 24-hour clock, currency.

Calculator
  • Bracket everything you'd group mentally — e.g. \(\dfrac{4+3}{2-5}\), not \(4+3/2-5\)
  • Use Ans and the fraction key; avoid copying decimals back in
  • For exact surds/trig, don't round until the final step
Time
\(1\text{ min} = 60\text{ s}\) · \(1\text{ h} = 60\text{ min}\) · \(1\text{ day} = 24\text{ h}\) · \(1\text{ year} = 365\text{ days}\) (366 in leap year)
24-hour clock 10:45 → 14:15 is 3 h 30 min \(= 3.5\text{ h} = 210\text{ min}\). Be careful crossing midnight.
Money & currency
Convertamount \(\times\) exchange rate (check direction!)
Example £250 at \(1.15\text{ €/£}\) → \(250 \times 1.15 = €287.50\). Coming back: \(€287.50 \div 1.15 = £250\).
Decimal pennies Always give money answers to 2 dp. \(3.7 \to 3.70\) — write the trailing zero.
