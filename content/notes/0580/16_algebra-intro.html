Letters stand in for unknown numbers. Every operation you can do with numbers, you can do with letters — just follow BIDMAS.

Notation
  • 3a means 3 × a · ab means a × b · a² means a × a
  • Drop the × sign; keep the number in front: 2 × x × y = 2xy
  • The coefficient of x² in 5x² + 3x is 5
Substitution
Replace every letter with its value, then evaluate with BIDMAS.
Example Given a = 3, b = −2, evaluate 2a² − 3b: 2(3)² − 3(−2) = 18 + 6 = 24.
Formula use v = u + at. u = 3, a = 9.8, t = 2 → v = 3 + 9.8 × 2 = 22.6.
Brackets for negatives Substituting a negative? Wrap it in brackets: (−2)² = 4, not −2² = −4.
