Letters stand in for unknown numbers. Every operation you can do with numbers, you can do with letters — just follow BIDMAS.

Notation
  • \(3a\) means \(3 \times a\) · \(ab\) means \(a \times b\) · \(a^{2}\) means \(a \times a\)
  • Drop the \(\times\) sign; keep the number in front: \(2 \times x \times y = 2xy\)
  • The coefficient of \(x^{2}\) in \(5x^{2} + 3x\) is \(5\)
Substitution
Replace every letter with its value, then evaluate with BIDMAS.
Example Given \(a = 3\), \(b = -2\), evaluate \(2a^{2} - 3b\): \(2(3)^{2} - 3(-2) = 18 + 6 = 24\).
Formula use \(v = u + at\). \(u = 3\), \(a = 9.8\), \(t = 2\) → \(v = 3 + 9.8 \times 2 = 22.6\).
Brackets for negatives Substituting a negative? Wrap it in brackets: \((-2)^{2} = 4\), not \(-2^{2} = -4\).
