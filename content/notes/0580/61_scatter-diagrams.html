Scatter diagrams show the relationship between two quantitative variables. Look for correlation, then add a line of best fit if asked.

Correlation
Positiveas x ↑, y ↑
Negativeas x ↑, y ↓
Noneno pattern — points scattered
Strength
  • Strong: points cluster tight around an imaginary line
  • Moderate: still a trend but noisier
  • Weak / none: no visible trend
Line of best fit
Single straight line; roughly equal points above and below; passes through the mean (x̄, ȳ).
Example Scatter of hours revised vs exam score shows strong positive correlation. Line of best fit lets you predict scores for unseen hours (within the data range only).
Correlation ≠ causation A trend on a scatter plot doesn't prove cause. Always state the correlation type + strength — examiners don't accept "they're related".
