Three averages. Pick the one that represents the data best given outliers and shape.

Mean\(\dfrac{\text{sum}}{\text{count}}\)
Medianmiddle value (sort first)
Modemost frequent
Range\(\max - \min\)
Median position
\(\dfrac{n + 1}{2}\). If \(n\) is even, average the middle two.
Grouped data (Extended)
Estimated mean\(\dfrac{\sum (f \times \text{midpoint})}{\sum f}\)
Modal classclass with highest frequency density (not raw frequency)
Example Data: \(3, 7, 7, 8, 15\). Mean \(= \tfrac{40}{5} = 8\). Median \(= 7\). Mode \(= 7\). Range \(= 15 - 3 = 12\).
Outlier effect One extreme value shifts the mean a lot, the median barely. Use median when data is skewed.
