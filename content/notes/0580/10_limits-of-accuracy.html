A rounded value hides a range. Bounds are the smallest/largest values that would round to it.

Finding bounds
\(x\) rounded to the nearest \(10 = 70\) → \(65 \le x < 75\). UB \(= 75\), LB \(= 65\).
\(x = 4.2\) to 1 dp → \(4.15 \le x < 4.25\)
Operations with bounds (Extended)
Max(a + b)\(\text{UB}(a) + \text{UB}(b)\)
Max(a − b)\(\text{UB}(a) - \text{LB}(b)\)
Max(a × b)\(\text{UB}(a) \times \text{UB}(b)\)
Max(a ÷ b)\(\text{UB}(a) \div \text{LB}(b)\)
Example Length \(= 12\text{ cm}\) (2 sf): \(11.5 \le L < 12.5\). Square perimeter: LB \(= 4 \times 11.5 = 46\), UB \(= 4 \times 12.5 = 50\).
Subtraction trap For \(a - b\) use \(\text{UB}(a) - \text{LB}(b)\), not \(\text{UB}(a) - \text{UB}(b)\). Flip the minuend's bound.
