A rounded value hides a range. Bounds are the smallest/largest values that would round to it.

Finding bounds
x rounded to the nearest 10 = 70 → 65 ≤ x < 75. UB = 75, LB = 65.
x = 4.2 to 1 dp → 4.15 ≤ x < 4.25
Operations with bounds (Extended)
Max(a + b)UB(a) + UB(b)
Max(a − b)UB(a) − LB(b)
Max(a × b)UB(a) × UB(b)
Max(a ÷ b)UB(a) ÷ LB(b)
Example Length = 12 cm (2 sf): 11.5 ≤ L < 12.5. Square perimeter: LB = 4 × 11.5 = 46, UB = 4 × 12.5 = 50.
Subtraction trap For a − b use UB(a) − LB(b), not UB(a) − UB(b). Flip the minuend's bound.
