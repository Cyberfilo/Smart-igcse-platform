(Extended.) Conditional probability: likelihood of B given A has already happened.

ConditionalP(B | A) = P(A ∩ B) / P(A)
Without-replacement scenario
Drawing without replacement naturally gives conditional probabilities — the second draw depends on the first.
Example Bag: 4 red, 6 blue. Pick 2 without replacement. P(2nd is red | 1st is red) = 3/9 = ⅓ (one less red, one less total).
Example (Venn) 30 students: 18 take French, 12 take German, 6 take both. P(French | German) = 6/12 = ½.
Independent test Events A and B are independent ⇔ P(B | A) = P(B). If knowing A happened changes P(B), they are dependent.
