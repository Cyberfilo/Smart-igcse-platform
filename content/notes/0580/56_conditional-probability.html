(Extended.) Conditional probability: likelihood of \(B\) given \(A\) has already happened.

Conditional\(P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}\)
Without-replacement scenario
Drawing without replacement naturally gives conditional probabilities — the second draw depends on the first.
Example Bag: \(4\) red, \(6\) blue. Pick \(2\) without replacement. \(P(\text{2nd is red} \mid \text{1st is red}) = \tfrac{3}{9} = \tfrac{1}{3}\) (one less red, one less total).
Example (Venn) \(30\) students: \(18\) take French, \(12\) take German, \(6\) take both. \(P(\text{French} \mid \text{German}) = \tfrac{6}{12} = \tfrac{1}{2}\).
Independent test Events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent \(\Leftrightarrow P(B \mid A) = P(B)\). If knowing \(A\) happened changes \(P(B)\), they are dependent.
