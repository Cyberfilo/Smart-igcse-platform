A sketch is NOT a plot. Show the shape + key coordinates (intercepts, turning points, asymptotes) — no grid, no scale.

Checklist for any sketch
  • Intercepts: where does it cross the axes?
  • Turning points: max, min, or stationary?
  • Asymptotes: what lines does it approach but never touch?
  • End behaviour: as x → ±∞, where does y go?
Quadratics
a > 0 → ∪ (min) · a < 0 → ∩ (max) · roots: factorise or use formula
Cubics
Up to 2 turning points. Lead term dominates as x → ±∞.
Sketch y = (x−1)(x+2)(x−3) Roots at x = 1, −2, 3. Lead x³ → ends bottom-left to top-right. Two turning points between roots. Cuts y-axis at (0, 6).
Reciprocal shape y = k/x: two branches. k > 0 → top-right + bottom-left. k < 0 → top-left + bottom-right. Asymptotes on both axes.
