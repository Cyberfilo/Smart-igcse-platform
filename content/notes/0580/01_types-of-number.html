Subsets
  • Natural \(\mathbb{N}\): \(\{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}\) · Integers \(\mathbb{Z}\): \(\{\ldots, -1, 0, 1, \ldots\}\)
  • Primes: \(2, 3, 5, 7, 11, \ldots\) (divisible only by \(1\) and itself; \(1\) is NOT prime)
  • Square numbers: \(1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, \ldots\)
  • Cube numbers: \(1, 8, 27, 64, 125, \ldots\)
Prime factorisation Every integer \(> 1\) factors uniquely into primes. Use for HCF (product of common primes) and LCM (product of all primes to highest power).
