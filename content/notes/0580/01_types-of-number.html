Every IGCSE number is in exactly one set: natural, integer, rational, or irrational.

Subsets
  • Natural ℕ: {1, 2, 3, …} · Integers ℤ: {…, −1, 0, 1, …}
  • Primes: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, … (divisible only by 1 and itself; 1 is NOT prime)
  • Square numbers: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, …
  • Cube numbers: 1, 8, 27, 64, 125, …
Prime factorisation Every integer > 1 factors uniquely into primes. Use for HCF (product of common primes) and LCM (product of all primes to highest power).
