In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides.

Pythagorasa² + b² = c²  (c = hypotenuse)
Finding each side
Find hypotenusec = √(a² + b²)
Find other sidea = √(c² − b²)
Example Sides 3 and 4 meet at a right angle → hypotenuse = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5.
3D example (Ext) Cuboid 3 × 4 × 12. Space diagonal = √(3² + 4² + 12²) = √169 = 13.
Is it right-angled? Test the converse: if a² + b² = c² then the triangle IS right-angled (opposite the longest side). Useful for proofs.
