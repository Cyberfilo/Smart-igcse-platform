In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides.

Pythagoras\(a^{2} + b^{2} = c^{2}\)  (\(c\) = hypotenuse)
Finding each side
Find hypotenuse\(c = \sqrt{a^{2} + b^{2}}\)
Find other side\(a = \sqrt{c^{2} - b^{2}}\)
Example Sides \(3\) and \(4\) meet at a right angle → hypotenuse \(= \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5\).
3D example (Ext) Cuboid \(3 \times 4 \times 12\). Space diagonal \(= \sqrt{3^{2} + 4^{2} + 12^{2}} = \sqrt{169} = 13\).
Is it right-angled? Test the converse: if \(a^{2} + b^{2} = c^{2}\) then the triangle IS right-angled (opposite the longest side). Useful for proofs.
