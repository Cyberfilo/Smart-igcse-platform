(Extended.) Two quantities vary together in a fixed-pattern relationship. Always substitute the given pair to find the constant k first.

Patterns
Directy ∝ x → y = kx
Inversey ∝ 1/x → y = k/x
Squarey ∝ x² → y = kx²
Inverse squarey ∝ 1/x² → y = k/x²
Square rooty ∝ √x → y = k√x
Cubey ∝ x³ → y = kx³
Example (direct square) y ∝ x²; y = 18 when x = 3. 18 = k(9) → k = 2. So y = 2x². When x = 5: y = 50.
Example (inverse) Pressure P is inversely proportional to volume V. P = 200 when V = 4 → k = 800 → P = 800/V.
Graph shapes Direct: straight line through origin. Inverse: hyperbola. Direct square: parabola through origin.
