(Extended.) Two quantities vary together in a fixed-pattern relationship. Always substitute the given pair to find the constant \(k\) first.

Patterns
Direct\(y \propto x \Rightarrow y = kx\)
Inverse\(y \propto \dfrac{1}{x} \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{k}{x}\)
Square\(y \propto x^{2} \Rightarrow y = kx^{2}\)
Inverse square\(y \propto \dfrac{1}{x^{2}} \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{k}{x^{2}}\)
Square root\(y \propto \sqrt{x} \Rightarrow y = k\sqrt{x}\)
Cube\(y \propto x^{3} \Rightarrow y = kx^{3}\)
Example (direct square) \(y \propto x^{2}\); \(y = 18\) when \(x = 3\). \(18 = k(9)\) → \(k = 2\). So \(y = 2x^{2}\). When \(x = 5\): \(y = 50\).
Example (inverse) Pressure \(P\) is inversely proportional to volume \(V\). \(P = 200\) when \(V = 4\) → \(k = 800\) → \(P = \dfrac{800}{V}\).
Graph shapes Direct: straight line through origin. Inverse: hyperbola. Direct square: parabola through origin.
