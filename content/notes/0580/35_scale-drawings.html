Scale tells you how map distance relates to real distance. 1 : n means 1 unit on paper = n real units.

Real distancemap distance × scale factor
Map distancereal distance ÷ scale factor
Example Scale 1 : 25 000. 4.8 cm on map → 4.8 × 25 000 = 120 000 cm = 1.2 km.
Bearings on a scale drawing
  • Bearings measured clockwise from North, always 3 digits (045°, not 45°)
  • Use a protractor, not a ruler angle
  • For two-leg bearings questions: draw North at each turning point
Example Ship sails 8 km on bearing 060°, then 5 km on bearing 150°. Draw carefully with scale 1 cm = 1 km. Measure final distance + bearing from start.
Match units Convert everything to the same unit before scaling. 25 000 cm = 250 m = 0.25 km — easy to forget.
