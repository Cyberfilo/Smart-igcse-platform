Interest earned on principal plus previously earned interest. Same formula handles growth and decay.

Growth\(A = P\!\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{100}\right)^{\!n}\)
Depreciation / decay\(A = P\!\left(1 - \dfrac{r}{100}\right)^{\!n}\)
Simple interest (compare)\(I = \dfrac{P \times r \times n}{100}\)
Percentage change
% change\(\dfrac{\text{new} - \text{original}}{\text{original}} \times 100\)
Reverse %original \(=\) amount \(\div\) multiplier
Compound £2000 at \(5\%\) for 3 years → \(A = 2000 \times 1.05^{3} = £2315.25\). Interest \(= £315.25\).
Reverse % After a \(20\%\) discount an item costs £64. Original \(= 64 \div 0.8 = £80\).
  • Non-annual compounding: divide \(r\) by periods/year, multiply \(n\) by periods/year
  • Interest earned \(= A - P\)
  • Never use successive simple interest where compound is asked
