Compare numbers using \(<\), \(>\), \(\le\), \(\ge\). Use a common form (all decimals, or all same-denominator fractions) before comparing.

Symbols
Strict\(a < b\) · \(a > b\)
Inclusive\(a \le b\) · \(a \ge b\)
Ordering mixed numbers
  • Convert all to decimals (most reliable)
  • Or give fractions a common denominator
  • For negative numbers: the larger the magnitude, the smaller the value (\(-7 < -3\))
Example Put \(\tfrac{1}{2}\), \(0.45\), \(40\%\), \(\tfrac{2}{3}\) in ascending order. Decimals: \(0.5, 0.45, 0.4, 0.667\). Order: \(40\% < 0.45 < \tfrac{1}{2} < \tfrac{2}{3}\).
Shortcut For two fractions \(\tfrac{a}{b}\) and \(\tfrac{c}{d}\), cross-multiply: \(a\,d\) vs \(c\,b\). \(\tfrac{a}{b} > \tfrac{c}{d}\) iff \(a\,d > c\,b\) (with positive denominators).
