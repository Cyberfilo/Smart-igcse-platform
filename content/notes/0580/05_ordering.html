Compare numbers using <, >, ≤, ≥. Use a common form (all decimals, or all same-denominator fractions) before comparing.

Symbols
Stricta < b · a > b
Inclusivea ≤ b · a ≥ b
Ordering mixed numbers
  • Convert all to decimals (most reliable)
  • Or give fractions a common denominator
  • For negative numbers: the larger the magnitude, the smaller the value (−7 < −3)
Example Put ½, 0.45, 40%, ⅔ in ascending order. Decimals: 0.5, 0.45, 0.4, 0.667. Order: 40% < 0.45 < ½ < ⅔.
Shortcut For two fractions a/b and c/d, cross-multiply: a·d vs c·b. a/b > c/d iff a·d > c·b (with positive denominators).
