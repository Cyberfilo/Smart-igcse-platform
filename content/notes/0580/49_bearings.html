Bearings measure direction. Always three digits, always clockwise from North.

Rules
  • Three-figure bearing: \(045°, 120°, 300°\) (never \(45°\) or \(12°\))
  • Measured clockwise from North
  • Draw a North line at every point you're measuring from
  • Back-bearing from \(B\) to \(A\) \(=\) bearing \(A\) to \(B\) \(\pm 180°\)
Example \(A\) is on a bearing \(070°\) from \(B\). Then \(B\) is on bearing \(070° + 180° = 250°\) from \(A\).
With sine/cosine rule Ship sails \(8\text{ km}\) on bearing \(060°\), then \(5\text{ km}\) on bearing \(150°\). Angle between legs \(= 150 - 60 = 90°\). Final distance \(= \sqrt{64 + 25} = \sqrt{89} \approx 9.43\text{ km}\) by Pythagoras.
Common mistake Don't measure bearings from where you want to go — measure from where you are. "Bearing from \(A\)" means \(A\) is the vertex.
