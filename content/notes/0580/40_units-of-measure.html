Convert between metric units by sliding the decimal point. Unit errors lose the whole mark.

Metric conversions
Length\(1\text{ km} = 1000\text{ m}\) · \(1\text{ m} = 100\text{ cm}\) · \(1\text{ cm} = 10\text{ mm}\)
Area\(1\text{ m}^{2} = 10\,000\text{ cm}^{2}\) · \(1\text{ cm}^{2} = 100\text{ mm}^{2}\)
Volume\(1\text{ m}^{3} = 10^{6}\text{ cm}^{3}\) · \(1\text{ cm}^{3} = 1\text{ ml}\)
Capacity\(1\text{ l} = 1000\text{ ml}\) · \(1\text{ m}^{3} = 1000\text{ l}\)
Mass\(1\text{ t} = 1000\text{ kg}\) · \(1\text{ kg} = 1000\text{ g}\)
Time\(1\text{ h} = 3600\text{ s}\) · \(1\text{ day} = 86\,400\text{ s}\)
Example Convert \(2.5\text{ m}^{3}\) to cm\(^{3}\): \(2.5 \times 10^{6} = 2\,500\,000\text{ cm}^{3}\).
Squared / cubed Area factor \(=\) (length factor)\(^{2}\). Volume factor \(=\) (length factor)\(^{3}\). Not the same factor — \(1\text{ m} = 100\text{ cm}\), BUT \(1\text{ m}^{2} = 10\,000\text{ cm}^{2}\).
