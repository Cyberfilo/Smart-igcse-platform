Ruler + compass constructions. Keep ALL arcs visible — examiners give method marks for the intersecting arcs.

Three essentials
  • Perpendicular bisector of AB: set compass > ½|AB|, draw arcs from A and B above and below; join intersections
  • Angle bisector: from the vertex, draw arc crossing both rays; from those two points draw intersecting arcs in the angle; join vertex to intersection
  • Perpendicular from a point to a line: from P, arc crossing line at two points; from those two, intersecting arcs below line; join P to intersection
Triangle SSS construction
Draw base. From endpoint A arc of length b. From endpoint B arc of length a. Intersection = third vertex C.
Loci "Points equidistant from A and B" = perpendicular bisector of AB. "Points equidistant from two rays" = angle bisector.
Leave the arcs Don't rub out construction arcs — they ARE your working. No arcs = no method mark, even if the final line is correct.
