Circle theorems let you find angles inside circles without measurement. Name the theorem — method marks depend on it.

Core theorems
  • Angle at centre \(= 2 \times\) angle at circumference (same arc)
  • Angles in the same segment are equal
  • Angle in a semicircle \(= 90°\)
  • Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral add to \(180°\)
  • Tangent \(\perp\) radius at the point of contact
  • Tangents from an external point are equal in length
Alternate segment theorem (Extended)
Angle between tangent and chord \(=\) angle in the alternate segment
Example In a circle with centre \(O\), angle at centre \(\angle AOB = 140°\). Then the angle at circumference \(\angle ACB\) (on the major arc) \(= 70°\).
Name the theorem In your working write "angle at centre \(= 2 \times\) angle at circumference". The theorem name is the method mark — not just the number.
