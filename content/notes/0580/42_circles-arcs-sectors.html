Circle basics everywhere. Arc and sector are just fractions of the full circle based on the angle θ.

Whole circle
CircumferenceC = 2πr = πd
AreaA = πr²
Arc & sector (angle θ in degrees)
Arc lengthL = (θ/360) × 2πr
Sector areaA = (θ/360) × πr²
Segment (Extended)
Segment area = sector area − triangle area = (θ/360)πr² − ½r² sin θ
Example Sector with r = 10, θ = 72°. Arc = (72/360)(2π·10) = 4π ≈ 12.57. Area = (72/360)(π·100) = 20π ≈ 62.83.
Use π exactly Keep π symbolic until the last step — answers in "terms of π" are often required, and rounding early loses accuracy.
