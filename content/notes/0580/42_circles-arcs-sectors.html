Circle basics everywhere. Arc and sector are just fractions of the full circle based on the angle \(\theta\).

Whole circle
Circumference\(C = 2\pi r = \pi d\)
Area\(A = \pi r^{2}\)
Arc & sector (angle \(\theta\) in degrees)
Arc length\(L = \dfrac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi r\)
Sector area\(A = \dfrac{\theta}{360} \times \pi r^{2}\)
Segment (Extended)
Segment area \(=\) sector area \(-\) triangle area \(= \dfrac{\theta}{360}\pi r^{2} - \tfrac{1}{2}r^{2}\sin\theta\)
Example Sector with \(r = 10\), \(\theta = 72°\). Arc \(= \tfrac{72}{360}(2\pi \cdot 10) = 4\pi \approx 12.57\). Area \(= \tfrac{72}{360}(\pi \cdot 100) = 20\pi \approx 62.83\).
Use π exactly Keep \(\pi\) symbolic until the last step — answers in "terms of \(\pi\)" are often required, and rounding early loses accuracy.
