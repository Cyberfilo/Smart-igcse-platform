(Extended.) Distance and midpoint of a segment from its endpoints — two pocket formulas.

Length
\(|AB| = \sqrt{(x_{2} - x_{1})^{2} + (y_{2} - y_{1})^{2}}\)
Midpoint
\(M = \left(\dfrac{x_{1} + x_{2}}{2},\ \dfrac{y_{1} + y_{2}}{2}\right)\)
Example \(A = (1, 2)\), \(B = (7, 10)\). Length \(= \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10\). Midpoint \(= (4, 6)\).
Reverse If midpoint of \(AB\) is \((3, 5)\) and \(A = (1, 2)\), then \(B = (2 \cdot 3 - 1,\ 2 \cdot 5 - 2) = (5, 8)\).
Length = √ of Pythagoras The distance formula IS Pythagoras' theorem on the horizontal & vertical distances. Always positive — take the magnitude.
