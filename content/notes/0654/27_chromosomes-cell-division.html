Chromosomes are coiled DNA. Genes are sections of DNA coding for specific proteins. Alleles are different versions of the same gene.

Human chromosomes
  • 46 chromosomes = 23 pairs
  • 22 pairs of autosomes + 1 pair sex chromosomes (XX female, XY male)
  • Haploid gametes carry 23
Mitosis
  • Growth, repair, asexual reproduction
  • Parent cell → 2 identical daughter cells (both diploid)
  • DNA replicates before division
Meiosis
  • Makes gametes (sperm, eggs)
  • One diploid parent cell → 4 haploid gametes
  • Two divisions; chromosomes crossover → genetic variation
Sex determination Father's sperm carries X or Y (50:50). Mother's egg always carries X. X sperm + X egg = XX (girl). Y sperm + X egg = XY (boy).
DNA terms Chromosome → gene → allele. DNA is a double helix made of base pairs (A-T, C-G).
