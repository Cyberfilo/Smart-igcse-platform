Power = rate of energy transfer. In circuits, P = IV.

PowerP = I × V
EnergyE = P × t = I × V × t
Alt form (Ext)P = I² × R = V² / R
Paying for electricity
Domestic bill = number of kWh × cost per kWh. 1 kWh = energy used by a 1 kW appliance running for 1 hour.
Example A 2 kW heater runs 3 hours. Energy = 2 × 3 = 6 kWh. At 20 p/kWh, cost = 120 p = £1.20.
Safety
  • Earthing — spare wire sends stray current safely to ground, trips fuse
  • Fuse — thin wire melts when current too high, breaks circuit
  • Circuit breakers — switch off automatically, resettable
  • Double insulation — two layers of plastic, no earthing needed
Fuse rating Choose fuse slightly higher than normal current. Iron 1000 W / 230 V → I = 4.3 A → 5 A fuse. Too low → keeps blowing; too high → doesn't protect.
