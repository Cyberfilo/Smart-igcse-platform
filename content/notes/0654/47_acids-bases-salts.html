Acids release H⁺ in water; alkalis release OH⁻. Reacting them gives a salt + water (neutralisation).

pH scale
0 (strong acid) · 7 (neutral) · 14 (strong alkali) · use universal indicator or pH meter
Typical reactions
  • Acid + base → salt + water
  • Acid + metal → salt + H₂ (more reactive metals)
  • Acid + carbonate → salt + CO₂ + water
  • Acid + alkali → salt + water (ionic: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O)
Examples HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O. H₂SO₄ + Mg → MgSO₄ + H₂. 2HNO₃ + CaCO₃ → Ca(NO₃)₂ + CO₂ + H₂O.
Salt preparation (soluble salt, metal + acid)
  1. Add excess metal/carbonate/base to warm acid until no more reacts
  2. Filter off excess solid
  3. Evaporate filtrate to saturation
  4. Cool to crystallise — collect and dry
Strong vs concentrated Strong = fully dissociates (HCl, NaOH). Concentrated = lots of solute per volume. They're independent — dilute HCl is still strong.
