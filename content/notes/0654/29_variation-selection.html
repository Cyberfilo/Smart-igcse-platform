Variation = differences between individuals. Natural selection acts on variation to produce evolution.

Types of variation
Continuous
  • Range of values (height, weight)
  • Genetic + environmental
  • Plotted as histogram
Discontinuous
  • Distinct categories (blood type, eye colour)
  • Mostly genetic
  • Plotted as bar chart
Natural selection (Darwin)
  1. More offspring produced than can survive
  2. Individuals vary (random mutations)
  3. Variation affects survival (selection pressure)
  4. Best-adapted survive → reproduce → pass on alleles
  5. Over generations, favourable alleles become common
Antibiotic resistance Bacteria with a resistance allele survive antibiotic treatment → multiply → population becomes resistant. Textbook natural selection in weeks.
Artificial selection Humans breed animals/plants with desired traits — same mechanism, human-chosen selection pressure (dogs from wolves, crops).
