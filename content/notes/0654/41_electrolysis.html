Electrolysis = using electricity to decompose an ionic substance (molten or in solution).

Setup
  • Electrolyte — the molten / dissolved ionic compound
  • Two electrodes: cathode (−) and anode (+)
  • Power supply drives electrons
At the electrodes
Cathode (−)attracts cations → reduction
Anode (+)attracts anions → oxidation
Molten NaCl Cathode: Na⁺ + e⁻ → Na. Anode: 2Cl⁻ → Cl₂ + 2e⁻. Products: sodium metal and chlorine gas.
Aqueous solutions (rules)
  • Cathode: H or the metal — less reactive wins (e.g. Cu over H; but H over Na)
  • Anode: O or the halide — halides win if concentrated; O₂ otherwise
Electroplating Object to be plated = cathode. Pure metal = anode. Electrolyte contains the metal's ions. e.g. silver-plating a spoon.
