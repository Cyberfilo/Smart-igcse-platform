Two strategies: asexual (one parent, identical offspring) vs sexual (two parents, genetic variation).

Asexual
  • One parent
  • Mitosis only
  • Genetically identical clones
  • Fast, no partner needed
  • No variation → whole population vulnerable
Sexual
  • Two parents
  • Gametes fuse (fertilisation)
  • Genetic variation → adaptation
  • Slower, resource-expensive
  • Meiosis produces haploid gametes
Mitosis vs meiosis
Mitosis1 division → 2 diploid (identical) cells
Meiosis2 divisions → 4 haploid (gametes), genetically different
Diploid vs haploid Diploid (2n): full chromosome pairs (46 in humans). Haploid (n): half-set (23 in gametes). Fertilisation restores 2n.
