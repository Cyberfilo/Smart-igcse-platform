Rate = how fast reactants turn into products. Collision theory: particles must collide with enough energy and correct orientation.

Rate factors
  • Temperature — particles move faster, more + harder collisions
  • Concentration — more particles in a volume, more collisions
  • Pressure (gases) — same effect as concentration
  • Surface area — powder reacts faster than lump
  • Catalyst — lowers activation energy, not consumed
Measuring rate
Gas producedvolume in a syringe vs time
Mass lostbalance + cotton wool
Colour changestopwatch for a mark to disappear
Example Mg + HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂. Plot volume of H₂ vs time. Steeper gradient = faster rate. Rate eventually drops to zero when a reactant runs out.
Catalyst Catalyst provides a reaction pathway with lower Ea. It is NOT used up — same mass before and after.
