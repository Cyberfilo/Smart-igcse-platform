Mass is how much matter (kg). Weight is the force of gravity on mass (N). Density = mass per unit volume.

WeightW = m × g
Densityρ = m / V
g values
  • Earth surface: g ≈ 9.8 N/kg (10 N/kg for approximations)
  • Moon: g ≈ 1.6 N/kg → same mass, ~⅙ weight
Measuring density
Regular solidmass balance + ruler (V = l × w × h)
Irregular solidmass balance + displacement in measuring cylinder
Liquidmass before vs after pouring into cylinder
Floats or sinks Density of water = 1.0 g/cm³. If an object's density < 1 → floats. Aluminium ρ = 2.7 g/cm³ → sinks. Wood ρ ≈ 0.5 g/cm³ → floats.
Unit watch ρ in g/cm³ or kg/m³ (note: 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³). Don't mix units.
