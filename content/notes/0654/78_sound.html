Sound is a longitudinal wave — compressions and rarefactions travelling through a medium. Needs a medium; cannot travel in a vacuum.

Speed in different media
Air~330 m/s
Water~1500 m/s
Steel~5000 m/s
Vacuum0 (no medium)
Pitch and loudness
  • Pitch ↔ frequency (higher f = higher pitch)
  • Loudness ↔ amplitude
  • Human hearing range: ~20 Hz to ~20 000 Hz (ultrasound above)
Echoes + ultrasound
distance = ½ × v × t (out and back; divide by 2)
Echo Shout, hear echo 0.6 s later. Wall distance = ½ × 330 × 0.6 = 99 m.
  • Ultrasound — medical imaging (prenatal scans), sonar, industrial cleaning
  • SONAR: measures sea depth using echo time
Cannot see on oscilloscope? Microphone converts sound to electrical signal. On an oscilloscope, sound wave shows as a transverse display even though the actual wave is longitudinal.
