Isotopes = atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons.

Same Z (protons) · Different A (neutrons) · Same chemistry · Different mass
Examples
  • Hydrogen: ¹H (protium), ²H (deuterium), ³H (tritium — radioactive)
  • Carbon: ¹²C (stable), ¹⁴C (radioactive, used for dating)
  • Chlorine: ³⁵Cl and ³⁷Cl (average gives relative atomic mass of 35.5)
Relative atomic mass
Weighted mean: Ar = (%₁ × A₁ + %₂ × A₂) / 100
Example Cl: 75% ³⁵Cl + 25% ³⁷Cl. Ar = (75×35 + 25×37)/100 = 35.5.
Radioactive isotopes
  • Unstable nucleus → decays emitting α, β, γ radiation
  • Medical use: iodine-131 for thyroid; cobalt-60 for cancer
  • Dating: C-14 for archaeology; U-238 for rocks
Same electrons All isotopes of one element have the same electron configuration, so they have the same chemistry.
