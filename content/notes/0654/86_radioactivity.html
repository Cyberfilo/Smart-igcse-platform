Unstable nuclei emit radiation: alpha (α), beta (β), gamma (γ). Random and unpredictable for individual atoms, but statistically regular.

Three types
α alpha2p + 2n (He nucleus) · +2 charge · heavy · stops on paper
β betahigh-speed e⁻ from neutron · −1 charge · stops on few mm Al
γ gammahigh-freq EM wave · 0 charge · stops on few cm lead
Decay equations
  • α: A decreases by 4, Z decreases by 2 · e.g. ²³⁸U → ²³⁴Th + ⁴He
  • β: A unchanged, Z increases by 1 · e.g. ¹⁴C → ¹⁴N + e⁻
  • γ: no change in A or Z (just energy released)
Ionisation + danger
  • α most ionising (most damage if internal), least penetrating
  • γ least ionising per interaction but goes deeper
  • β in between
  • Protection: distance, time, shielding
Background radiation Always present — cosmic rays, radon gas, rocks, medical scans, food. Subtract before quoting readings.
