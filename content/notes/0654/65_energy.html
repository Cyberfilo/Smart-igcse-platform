Energy is conserved — cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred between stores.

Energy stores
  • Kinetic — moving object
  • Gravitational potential — height above ground
  • Elastic — stretched/compressed spring
  • Thermal / internal — hot object
  • Chemical — food, fuel, batteries
  • Nuclear — atomic nucleus
  • Electrostatic / magnetic — fields
Key equations
KEE_k = ½mv²
GPEE_p = mgh
Conservation of energy
Total energy before = total energy after. Energy may transfer between stores but the sum is constant.
Falling ball At top: GPE = mgh, KE = 0. At bottom: GPE = 0, KE = ½mv². Equating: mgh = ½mv² → v = √(2gh).
Useful vs wasted Some energy always ends up as heat / sound — dissipated. Useful energy = energy transferred to intended store.
