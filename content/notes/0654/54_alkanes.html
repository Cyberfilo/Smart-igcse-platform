Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons — only single C-C bonds. General formula CₙH₂ₙ₊₂. Found in crude oil.

ButaneC₄H₁₀
Properties + reactions
  • Fairly unreactive (single bonds strong)
  • Combustion (complete): CₓHᵧ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (exothermic)
  • Incomplete combustion: produces CO and soot (C) — dangerous
  • Substitution with halogens (UV light needed): CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl
Crude oil + fractional distillation
Crude oil heated, vapours rise up a tower with decreasing temperature. Fractions separated by boiling point: gases, petrol, kerosene, diesel, bitumen.
Saturated "Saturated" means every carbon has its maximum number of H attached — all single bonds, no double bonds.
