Flowers are sex organs. Pollination + fertilisation = seed formation, which grows into a new plant.

Flower structure
  • Stamen (male): anther (makes pollen) + filament
  • Carpel (female): stigma (receives pollen) + style + ovary (contains ovules)
  • Petals — attract pollinators
  • Sepals — protect bud
Pollination
Insect-pollinated
  • Bright, scented petals
  • Nectar + nectaries
  • Sticky pollen
Wind-pollinated
  • Small, dull petals (or none)
  • No scent/nectar
  • Light, abundant pollen; feathery stigmas
Fertilisation
Pollen grain on stigma → pollen tube grows down style → male nucleus fuses with ovule → seed
Germination needs Water (activates enzymes), oxygen (respiration), warmth (optimum enzyme activity). Light is NOT needed for most seeds to germinate.
