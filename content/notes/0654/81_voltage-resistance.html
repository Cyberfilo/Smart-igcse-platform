Voltage (p.d.) is the energy per unit charge. Resistance opposes current.

Voltage (p.d.)V = E / Q (volts)
ResistanceR = V / I (Ohm's law)
Ohm's law
V = I × R (for an ohmic conductor at constant T)
Measuring V and I
  • Voltmeter — in PARALLEL across the component, high resistance
  • Ammeter — in SERIES with the component, low resistance
Resistance of a wire
  • ↑ length → ↑ resistance
  • ↑ cross-sectional area → ↓ resistance
  • ↑ temperature → ↑ resistance (metals)
Example V = 12 V, I = 0.5 A. R = V/I = 24 Ω.
Filament lamp Not ohmic. As current ↑, wire heats up, resistance ↑. V-I graph curves, not straight line.
