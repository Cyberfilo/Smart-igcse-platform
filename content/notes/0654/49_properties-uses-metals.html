Metals have predictable physical properties. Match the property to the right use.

Typical properties
  • Shiny when polished
  • Good conductors of heat + electricity
  • Malleable (can be hammered into shapes)
  • Ductile (can be drawn into wires)
  • High melting points (except Hg, Ga)
  • High density
  • Sonorous (ring when struck)
Property → use
Copperwires (conducts electricity well, ductile)
Aluminiumplanes, cans (low density + resists corrosion)
Iron/steelbridges, buildings (strong, cheap)
Goldjewellery, electronics (unreactive, conducts)
Non-metal contrast Non-metals: usually dull, poor conductors (except graphite), brittle solids or gases, low m.p. Sulfur, nitrogen, oxygen are all classic non-metals.
