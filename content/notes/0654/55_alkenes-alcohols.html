Alkenes have a C=C double bond (CₙH₂ₙ). Alcohols have an –OH functional group.

Alkenes
  • Unsaturated — have one C=C double bond
  • General formula: CₙH₂ₙ
  • Examples: ethene C₂H₄, propene C₃H₆, butene C₄H₈
Test for alkene (double bond)
Bromine water (orange) + alkene → decolourised (addition reaction). Alkanes do not decolourise.
Addition reactions (Extended)
+ H₂ethene → ethane (Ni catalyst)
+ H₂Oethene → ethanol (steam, H₃PO₄ catalyst)
+ Br₂ethene → 1,2-dibromoethane
Alcohols — ethanol
  • Functional group: –OH
  • Made by fermentation: C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ (yeast, anaerobic)
  • Or by addition: ethene + steam → ethanol
  • Combustion: C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O
Homologous series Alkanes, alkenes, alcohols — each series has a general formula, similar chemistry, and gradients of properties (b.p. up with more C).
