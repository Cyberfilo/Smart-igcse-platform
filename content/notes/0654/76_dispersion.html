White light is a mix of colours. A prism refracts each colour by a different amount → spreading into a spectrum.

Order of refraction
Red refracts LEAST · Violet refracts MOST. Order (ROYGBIV): Red · Orange · Yellow · Green · Blue · Indigo · Violet.
Why dispersion happens
  • Different colours = different wavelengths
  • Each wavelength has a slightly different speed in glass → different refractive index
  • Longer wavelength (red) bends less; shorter wavelength (violet) bends more
Natural examples
  • Rainbow — sunlight refracts + reflects inside raindrops
  • Prism spectroscope — used for chemical analysis
  • Soap bubbles — interference colours, not dispersion
Double prism A second prism inverted recombines the spectrum back into white light — proves the colours were there all along.
Electromagnetic spectrum extension Visible light is a small part of the EM spectrum. Beyond red is IR; beyond violet is UV. Dispersion demo hints at this breadth.
