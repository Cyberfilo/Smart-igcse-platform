Pressure = force per unit area. Same force, smaller area → bigger pressure.

PressureP = F / A (pascals)
In a liquidP = ρ × g × h
Units
  • 1 Pa = 1 N/m²
  • 1 kPa = 1000 Pa
  • atmospheric pressure ≈ 100 kPa at sea level
Everyday examples
  • Sharp knife: small area → high pressure → cuts easily
  • Wide tyres on soft ground: large area → low pressure → doesn't sink
  • Dam walls thicker at the bottom: pressure increases with depth
  • Ears pop in a plane: atmospheric pressure decreases with altitude
Example Diver 10 m deep in water. Extra pressure = 1000 × 10 × 10 = 100 000 Pa = 100 kPa. Total pressure = atmospheric + water = ~200 kPa.
Gas pressure Gas pressure comes from particle collisions with container walls. More collisions (higher T, smaller V) → higher pressure.
