Charge is measured in coulombs (C). Current is the rate of flow of charge.

CurrentI = Q / t (amperes)
ChargeQ = I × t
Charge and electrons
  • Electron charge = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
  • Charge is conserved
  • Conventional current: from + to − (opposite to electron flow)
Static electricity
  • Rub insulators → transfer electrons → one becomes + charged, the other −
  • Like charges repel; unlike charges attract
  • Examples: hair stands up from a balloon, lightning, dust on screens
Current in circuits
  • Measured with an ammeter, connected in series
  • Ammeters must have very low resistance
  • Current is the same everywhere in a series circuit
Example A 2 A current flows for 30 s. Q = It = 2 × 30 = 60 C. Number of electrons = 60 / 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ ≈ 3.75 × 10²⁰.
