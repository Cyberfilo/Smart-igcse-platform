Food travels through the alimentary canal; accessory organs secrete juices into it.

The canal, in order
  • Mouth — mechanical digestion (teeth), amylase in saliva
  • Oesophagus — peristalsis pushes food down
  • Stomach — protein digestion (pepsin), HCl kills bacteria
  • Small intestine — digestion completed + absorption (villi)
  • Large intestine — water reabsorbed, faeces formed
  • Rectum + anus — faeces stored, egested
Accessory organs
  • Liver — makes bile (emulsifies fats)
  • Gall bladder — stores bile
  • Pancreas — secretes amylase, protease, lipase into small intestine
  • Salivary glands — secrete saliva containing amylase
Mechanical vs chemical Teeth and stomach muscle do mechanical digestion (breaking up). Enzymes do chemical digestion (bond breaking). Both increase surface area.
