Reactions either release heat (exothermic) or absorb heat (endothermic). Energy change = bonds broken − bonds made.

Exothermic
  • Releases heat to surroundings (temperature rises)
  • ΔH is negative
  • Products lower energy than reactants
  • Examples: combustion, neutralisation, most oxidation
Endothermic
  • Absorbs heat (temperature drops)
  • ΔH is positive
  • Products higher energy than reactants
  • Examples: thermal decomposition, photosynthesis
Energy diagrams
Activation energy Ea = hump to climb before reactants become products. Catalyst lowers Ea.
Bond energies (Extended)
ΔH = Σ(bonds broken) − Σ(bonds made)    negative = exothermic
Example CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O. Bonds broken: 4 C-H + 2 O=O. Bonds made: 2 C=O + 4 O-H. ΔH comes out negative — combustion is exothermic.
