Keeping internal conditions constant — temperature, water, blood glucose — despite external changes. Uses negative feedback: a change triggers a reverse response.

Temperature regulation
  • Too hot: sweating (evaporation cools skin), vasodilation (more blood to skin, loses heat)
  • Too cold: shivering (generates heat), vasoconstriction (less blood to skin), hair erector muscles contract
Blood glucose regulation
Too highpancreas → insulin → liver converts glucose → glycogen
Too lowpancreas → glucagon → liver converts glycogen → glucose
Diabetes
  • Type 1: pancreas produces little/no insulin — treated with insulin injections
  • Type 2: cells become resistant to insulin — lifestyle + diet
Water balance Kidney regulates blood water via ADH from pituitary. Too little water → more ADH → kidney reabsorbs more water → less urine.
