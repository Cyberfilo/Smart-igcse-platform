A drug is any substance taken into the body that alters chemical processes. Medicinal drugs help; recreational drugs can harm.

Medicinal examples
  • Antibiotics — kill bacteria (not viruses). Over-use → resistant bacteria (MRSA)
  • Painkillers — block pain signals (ibuprofen, paracetamol)
  • Antivirals — slow viral replication
Alcohol
  • Depressant — slows nerve impulses
  • Impairs reaction time, judgement, coordination
  • Long-term: liver damage (cirrhosis), addiction
Tobacco / smoking
  • Nicotine — addictive stimulant
  • Tar — carcinogenic, coats alveoli, causes cancer
  • CO — binds haemoglobin irreversibly, reduces O₂ capacity
  • Smoke particles — destroy cilia → smoker's cough
Antibiotic resistance Caused by over-prescription + not completing the course. Evolved bacteria survive and multiply.
