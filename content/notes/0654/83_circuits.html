Two arrangements: series (one path) and parallel (multiple paths). Voltage and current rules differ sharply.

Series circuit
Currentsame everywhere
Voltagesplits across components: V_total = V₁ + V₂
ResistanceR_total = R₁ + R₂
Parallel circuit
Currentsplits into branches: I_total = I₁ + I₂
Voltagesame across each branch
Resistance1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ · total is LESS than smallest
Example (parallel) Two 6 Ω resistors in parallel: 1/R = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 → R = 3 Ω. Two in parallel halves the resistance.
Special components
  • Variable resistor (rheostat) — adjusts current
  • LDR — resistance falls in bright light
  • Thermistor — resistance falls at high temperature
  • Diode — current one direction only
Household wiring Houses use parallel circuits so each appliance has full voltage + one device failing doesn't kill the rest.
