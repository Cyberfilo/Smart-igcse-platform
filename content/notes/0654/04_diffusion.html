Net movement of particles from high to low concentration — passive, no energy needed.

Rate-affecting factors
  • Temperature — more KE → faster diffusion
  • Concentration gradient — steeper → faster
  • Surface area — larger → faster
  • Distance — shorter → faster
  • Size of particles — smaller → faster
Biology examples
  • O₂ from alveoli into blood capillaries
  • CO₂ out of cells during respiration
  • Dissolved glucose into villi in the small intestine
  • CO₂ into leaves and O₂ out during photosynthesis
Adaptations for diffusion Alveoli: many + moist + thin wall + good blood supply → large SA, short distance, steep gradient. Same four adaptations appear in gills and villi.
Passive Diffusion requires NO ATP. Compare active transport (against gradient, uses ATP).
