A hydrogen–oxygen fuel cell converts chemical energy directly to electrical energy. Only waste product is water.

Overall2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O + electrical energy
How it works
  • H₂ fed in at anode — electrons stripped off, H⁺ produced
  • Electrons flow through external circuit → power device
  • H⁺ migrates through electrolyte to cathode
  • At cathode: H⁺ + e⁻ + O₂ → H₂O
Advantages
+no pollution (water only)
+very efficient
+no recharging needed (refuel like petrol)
−H₂ is hard to store safely
−H₂ production may use fossil fuels
−expensive infrastructure
Fuel cell vs battery Battery: closed system, needs recharging. Fuel cell: open system, refuel continuously. Both are galvanic (chemical → electrical).
