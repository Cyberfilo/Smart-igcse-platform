Moments = turning effect of a force about a pivot.

MomentM = F × d (perpendicular distance from pivot)
Principle of moments
For equilibrium: total clockwise moments = total anticlockwise moments
Seesaw Child A (30 kg) sits 2 m from pivot. Child B (20 kg) must sit at distance d for balance. 30 × 2 = 20 × d → d = 3 m.
Equilibrium conditions
  1. No net force (translational equilibrium)
  2. No net moment (rotational equilibrium)
Everyday examples
  • Spanner — long handle = big moment for small force
  • Door handles far from hinges
  • Wheelbarrow — load close to wheel (pivot) → less effort
Perpendicular distance Always measure d perpendicular to the force's line of action. If the force is not perpendicular to the lever, resolve first.
