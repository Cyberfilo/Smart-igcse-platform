Physical changes: reversible, no new substance. Chemical changes: new substance, usually not easily reversible.

Physical
  • Melting, freezing, boiling
  • Dissolving salt in water
  • Cutting, crushing, bending
  • Reversible by cooling / evaporation
Chemical
  • Burning (combustion)
  • Rusting
  • Neutralisation
  • Cooking, digesting
Signs of chemical change
  • New substance formed
  • Colour change (not just dilution)
  • Gas given off (bubbles in non-boiling solution)
  • Precipitate forms
  • Heat or light released or absorbed
Tricky case: dissolving NaCl + water → salt solution is physical (NaCl recoverable by evaporation). But Mg + HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂ is chemical (H₂ gas released).
