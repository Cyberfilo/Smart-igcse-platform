Three states, three particle arrangements. Heating supplies energy for particles to overcome attractive forces.

Solid
  • Particles tightly packed, regular lattice
  • Vibrate in place
  • Fixed shape, fixed volume
Liquid
  • Particles close, irregular
  • Move around each other
  • Fixed volume, takes container shape
Gas
  • Particles far apart, no attractive forces
  • Move rapidly and randomly
  • Fills any container — no fixed shape or volume
Changes of state
solid →melting liquid →boiling/evap gas · reverse with freezing / condensation
Sublimation Solid → gas without passing through liquid (e.g. dry ice CO₂, iodine). Rare but examinable.
