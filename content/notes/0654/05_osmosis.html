Net movement of water from a dilute solution to a concentrated one, through a partially permeable membrane.

Dilute (high water potential) → Concentrated (low water potential)
Effects on cells
Animal cell
  • In dilute solution: swells, bursts (haemolysis)
  • In conc. solution: shrinks, crenated
Plant cell
  • In dilute: turgid — wall prevents bursting
  • In conc.: plasmolysed — membrane pulls from wall
Potato experiment Mass of potato chip in concentrated sugar solution DECREASES (water moves out). In pure water, mass INCREASES. No change = isotonic.
Water potential Pure water = highest water potential (0 kPa). Adding solute lowers it (negative values). Water flows from high to low.
