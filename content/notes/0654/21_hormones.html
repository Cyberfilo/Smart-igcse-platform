Hormones are chemical messengers produced by endocrine glands. They travel in blood to target organs and act more slowly than nerves, but longer-lasting.

Key hormones
Adrenalinegland: adrenal. Target: many. Prepares body for fight/flight
Insulingland: pancreas. Target: liver. Lowers blood glucose
Glucagongland: pancreas. Raises blood glucose
Oestrogengland: ovaries. Puberty + menstrual cycle
Testosteronegland: testes. Male puberty
ADHgland: pituitary. Regulates water balance
Adrenaline effects
  • ↑ heart rate
  • ↑ breathing rate
  • Widens pupils
  • Diverts blood from gut to muscles
  • Raises blood glucose
Hormone vs nerve Nerve: fast, short-lived, targeted (along a neurone). Hormone: slower, longer-lasting, widespread (via blood).
