All matter is made of particles in constant motion. Higher temperature = faster average motion.

Three states
  • Solid — particles tightly packed, vibrate in place, fixed shape and volume
  • Liquid — particles close but mobile, fixed volume, takes container shape
  • Gas — particles far apart, fast random motion, fills container
Temperature and KE
Higher T → higher average KE of particles → faster motion.
Brownian motion
Random zig-zag motion of small particles (e.g. smoke, pollen grains) — evidence of fast-moving, invisible gas/liquid particles colliding with them.
Gas pressure
  • Particles hit container walls → exert force → pressure
  • More particles per volume (higher density) → higher P
  • Higher T → faster motion → harder collisions → higher P
  • Smaller volume at constant T → more frequent collisions → higher P (Boyle's)
P ∝ 1/V Boyle's law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ at constant T. Halve V → double P. Standard IGCSE gas law.
