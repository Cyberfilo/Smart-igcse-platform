Polymers are very long molecules made from many repeated small units (monomers).

Addition polymerisation
Alkene monomers (C=C breaks) join together with no other product.
Poly(ethene) from ethene n CH₂=CH₂ → (-CH₂-CH₂-)ₙ. The C=C opens and links to the next monomer.
Common polymers + uses
Poly(ethene)plastic bags, bottles
Poly(propene)ropes, crates
Poly(chloroethene) PVCpipes, cables
Poly(tetrafluoroethene) PTFEnon-stick pans
Disposal problems
  • Most addition polymers are non-biodegradable → landfill / ocean for centuries
  • Burning releases toxic gases (e.g. HCl from PVC)
  • Solution: recycle, reduce use, develop biodegradable alternatives
Drawing the repeat unit Take one monomer. Replace the C=C with a single bond. Put brackets with "n" outside and bonds coming out of both ends. e.g. (-CH₂-CH₂-)ₙ.
