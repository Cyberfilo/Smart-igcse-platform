Reflection: wave bounces off. Refraction: wave bends when entering a new medium because speed changes.

Laws of reflection
  • Angle of incidence = angle of reflection (both measured from normal)
  • Incident ray, normal, reflected ray all in one plane
Refraction
  • Wave enters denser medium → slows down → bends TOWARD normal
  • Wave enters less dense medium → speeds up → bends AWAY from normal
  • Refractive index n = sin i / sin r (Snell's law)
  • Also: n = c (in vacuum) / v (in medium)
Example Light enters glass (n = 1.5) at 30° from air. sin r = sin 30° / 1.5 = 0.333 → r = 19.5°. Ray bends toward normal.
Total internal reflection
  • Happens when light tries to pass from denser to less dense medium
  • Critical angle c: sin c = 1/n
  • Beyond c, light is totally reflected (not refracted)
  • Used in optical fibres and prisms in binoculars
Apparent depth Fish in pond appears shallower than it is. Stick in water looks bent. Both due to refraction at air-water boundary.
