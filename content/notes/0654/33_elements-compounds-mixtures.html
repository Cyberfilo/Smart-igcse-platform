Three classes of substance — distinguished by whether atoms are of one kind, chemically bonded, or merely mixed.

Elementone type of atom (e.g. Cu, O₂)
Compound2+ elements chemically bonded (e.g. H₂O, NaCl)
Mixture2+ substances, not chemically combined (e.g. air, sea water)
Key differences
  • Compounds have fixed ratios; mixtures don't
  • Compounds have new properties; mixtures keep component properties
  • Compounds need a chemical reaction to separate; mixtures can be separated physically
Iron and sulfur Mixture: can separate with a magnet. Heat to react → iron(II) sulfide (compound, FeS). Non-magnetic, new properties.
"Pure" A pure substance is an element or a compound — one kind of particle. A mixture is impure even if it's 99.9%.
