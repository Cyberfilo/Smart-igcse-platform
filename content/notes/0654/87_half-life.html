Half-life = time for half the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay. Constant for a given isotope.

Exponential decay
After n half-lives: remaining = initial × (½)ⁿ
Example Isotope with t½ = 20 years. After 60 years (3 half-lives): 1/8 of the original remains. If you started with 80 mg, 10 mg remain.
Graphical method
  • Plot activity (or count rate) vs time
  • Read how long it takes to drop from any value to half of it
  • Should be the same from any starting point — that confirms the half-life
Uses of half-life
Long t½ (billions)uranium/rock dating
Medium (thousands)carbon-14 archaeological dating
Short (hours)medical tracers (min radiation dose)
Background subtraction Measured count rate includes background. Subtract background before calculating half-life — raw readings give a wrong answer.
