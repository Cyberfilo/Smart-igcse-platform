The mole is a counting unit. 1 mole = 6.02 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number).

Moles from massn = m / Mr
Mass from molesm = n × Mr
Moles of gas (rtp)n = V / 24 000 (cm³) or V / 24 (dm³)
Concentrationc = n / V (mol/dm³)
Steps for a calculation
  1. Write the balanced equation
  2. Convert given quantity to moles
  3. Use mole ratio to find moles of required
  4. Convert moles → required unit (mass, vol, conc)
Example Mass of MgO from 4.8 g Mg burning fully? n(Mg) = 4.8/24 = 0.2. Mole ratio Mg:MgO = 1:1 → 0.2 mol MgO. m = 0.2 × 40 = 8 g.
Mr vs Ar Ar = relative ATOMIC mass (single atom). Mr = relative MOLECULAR mass (whole compound). Mr(H₂O) = 2 + 16 = 18.
