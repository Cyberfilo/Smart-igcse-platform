The heart is a double pump. Humans have a double circulation — blood passes through the heart twice per lap.

Heart structure
  • Right atrium receives deoxygenated blood from vena cava
  • → Right ventricle → pulmonary artery → lungs
  • Left atrium receives oxygenated blood from pulmonary vein
  • → Left ventricle → aorta → body
  • Valves (tricuspid, bicuspid/mitral, semilunar) prevent backflow
Left vs right
Left ventricle has thicker muscular wall — has to pump blood around the whole body. Right ventricle only pumps to the lungs.
Coronary heart disease
  • Fatty deposits (cholesterol) build up in coronary arteries
  • Restrict blood supply to heart muscle
  • Risk factors: high-fat diet, smoking, lack of exercise, high BP, stress
  • Prevention: healthy diet, exercise, don't smoke
