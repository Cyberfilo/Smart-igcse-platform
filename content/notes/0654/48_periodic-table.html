Elements arranged by increasing atomic number. Vertical groups share valence electrons and properties; horizontal periods show trends.

Four key groups
  • Group I — Alkali metals (Li, Na, K): soft, very reactive, +1 ions, react with water
  • Group VII — Halogens (F, Cl, Br, I): coloured diatomic non-metals, −1 ions, displacement reactions
  • Group 0/VIII — Noble gases (He, Ne, Ar): full outer shell, nearly unreactive
  • Transition metals (Fe, Cu, Zn): high m.p., coloured compounds, variable valency, catalysts
Trends (Group I vs Group VII)
Group I, downmore reactive (e⁻ easier to lose)
Group VII, downless reactive (e⁻ harder to gain)
Group VII displacement Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂. More reactive Cl displaces less reactive Br.
Why the pattern? Group I: outer e⁻ further from nucleus down the group → easier to lose. Group VII: outer shell further from nucleus → harder to attract e⁻.
