Three classes of enzyme break three classes of food into small soluble molecules that can be absorbed.

AmylaseStarch → maltose
MaltaseMaltose → glucose
Protease (e.g. pepsin)Protein → amino acids
LipaseLipid → fatty acids + glycerol
Bile's role
Bile from liver emulsifies fats — breaks large drops into smaller ones, increasing SA for lipase. Not a digestive enzyme itself.
Absorption in the small intestine
  • Villi: millions of finger-like projections — massive SA
  • Microvilli on each villus — further SA increase
  • Thin epithelium — short diffusion path
  • Rich blood supply — maintains steep concentration gradient
  • Lacteal inside each villus absorbs fats
Low pH of stomach Optimum pH for pepsin is 1.5–2. Amylase needs pH 7, so it stops working in the stomach — restarts in small intestine after bile neutralises the acid.
