Redox reactions: one species is oxidised, another is reduced. Simultaneous — always paired.

OIL RIGOxidation Is Loss · Reduction Is Gain (of electrons)
Alternative definitions
Oxidationgain of O · loss of H · loss of e⁻
Reductionloss of O · gain of H · gain of e⁻
Oxidising vs reducing agents
  • Oxidising agent — oxidises another substance (itself gets reduced)
  • Reducing agent — reduces another substance (itself gets oxidised)
  • Common oxidising agents: O₂, Cl₂, dilute acid H⁺, KMnO₄
  • Common reducing agents: C, CO, H₂, Zn, Fe
Example (Extended) Zn + Cu²⁺ → Zn²⁺ + Cu. Zn loses electrons (oxidised); Cu²⁺ gains (reduced). Zn is the reducing agent, Cu²⁺ the oxidising agent.
In combustion Fuel is oxidised (gains O). O₂ is reduced (gains e⁻ forming O²⁻ in oxides). Every combustion is a redox reaction.
