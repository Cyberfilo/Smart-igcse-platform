Heating → particles vibrate/move more → greater average separation → expansion. Cooling reverses it.

Order of expansion (most → least)
Gas > Liquid > Solid (for the same temperature rise)
Everyday evidence
  • Railway tracks have expansion gaps
  • Bridges use expansion joints
  • Thermometers use expansion of alcohol or mercury in a narrow tube
  • Bimetallic strip bends on heating — used in thermostats
  • Power lines sag in summer, taut in winter
Anomalous water (Extended)
Water EXPANDS on freezing (unlike most substances). Ice floats on water → lakes don't freeze solid from bottom.
Bimetallic strip Brass expands more than iron. On heating, the strip bends with brass on the outside of the curve. Used to break electric circuits in safety switches.
Bursting pipes Water in pipes expands when freezing → breaks the pipe. Leak appears when ice melts. Common winter issue.
