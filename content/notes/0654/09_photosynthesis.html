Plants convert light energy into chemical energy (glucose). Happens in chloroplasts, in the mesophyll cells of leaves.

Word equationcarbon dioxide + water →light, chlorophyll glucose + oxygen
Symbol equation6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
Limiting factors
  • Light intensity — rate rises then plateaus
  • CO₂ concentration — usually limiting outdoors at 0.04%
  • Temperature — enzymes in photosynthesis denature above ~40 °C
  • Chlorophyll — can be reduced by deficiency (Mg²⁺) or disease
Graph reading As light rises, rate increases linearly then levels off. The plateau means another factor (CO₂ or temp) is now limiting.
Use of glucose Respiration (energy) · stored as starch · converted to cellulose (cell walls) · used to make amino acids (with N from NO₃⁻).
