Match the technique to what you're separating. Every technique exploits a physical difference (b.p., solubility, size).

Separation methods
  • Filtration — insoluble solid from liquid (sand from water)
  • Evaporation / crystallisation — soluble solid from solution (salt from salt water)
  • Simple distillation — pure solvent from solution (water from salt water)
  • Fractional distillation — miscible liquids with different b.p. (ethanol from water; crude oil fractions)
  • Magnetic — only for magnetic solids (iron from sulfur)
  • Chromatography — coloured solutes by different solubilities
Paper chromatography
Rfdistance moved by spot ÷ distance moved by solvent
Example Solvent front at 8 cm; spot at 2 cm. Rf = 2/8 = 0.25. Rf is constant for a given substance + solvent.
Origin line in pencil Always draw the origin line in pencil — pen ink dissolves and contaminates the chromatogram.
