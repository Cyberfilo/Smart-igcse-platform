A force changes speed, direction, or shape. Newton's second law links net force to acceleration.

Newton's 2ndF = m × a
Types of force
  • Weight (gravity, always downward)
  • Normal contact (perpendicular to surface)
  • Friction (opposes motion)
  • Tension (in strings/ropes)
  • Thrust / drive (engines)
  • Upthrust (in fluids)
Hooke's law
F = k × e (force proportional to extension, within limit of proportionality)
Spring example Spring constant k = 50 N/m. Load 10 N → extension = 10/50 = 0.2 m. Double load → double extension, if still within limit.
Terminal velocity
Falling object reaches steady v when drag = weight. Acceleration = 0. Parachute reduces terminal v by increasing surface area.
Balanced vs unbalanced Balanced forces: no change in motion. Unbalanced: acceleration in the direction of net force. Still motion if velocity ≠ 0; just constant.
