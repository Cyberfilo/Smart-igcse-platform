Phase changes involve energy to break/form particle bonds. Temperature stays constant during the change.

Key phase changes
Meltingsolid → liquid (energy absorbed)
Freezingliquid → solid (energy released)
Boilingliquid → gas throughout
Evaporationliquid → gas at surface, any T
Condensationgas → liquid
Boiling vs evaporation
  • Boiling: only at b.p. · from entire liquid · bubbles
  • Evaporation: any temperature · from surface only · no bubbles
  • Evaporation faster with: higher T, larger SA, wind, low humidity
Latent heat
Energy to change state with no T change — breaks particle bonds, not KE.
Heating curve Ice at −20 °C heated: T rises to 0 °C (steep), T stays flat during melting (energy = latent heat of fusion), T rises to 100 °C, flat during boiling, then rises again.
Cooling by evaporation Fast-moving particles leave the surface → average KE (and T) of remaining drops. Sweating works this way.
