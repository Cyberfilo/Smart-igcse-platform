Speed, velocity, acceleration — and the four equations linking them.

Definitions
Speeddistance / time (scalar)
Velocitydisplacement / time (vector — has direction)
Acceleration(v − u) / t
Equations of motion
v = u + at · s = ut + ½at² · v² = u² + 2as · s = ½(u + v)t
Motion graphs
  • d-t graph: gradient = velocity · flat = stationary · curve = changing speed
  • v-t graph: gradient = acceleration · area = distance
  • a-t graph: area = change in velocity
Free fall
g ≈ 9.8 m/s² (take as 10 m/s² for quick calcs). All objects fall at same rate in a vacuum.
Example Ball dropped from 20 m. v² = 0 + 2(10)(20) = 400. v = 20 m/s on impact. Time: 20 = 0 + 10t → t = 2 s.
