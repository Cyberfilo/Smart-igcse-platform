Movement of particles against the concentration gradient, through carrier proteins, using energy from respiration.

Directionlow → high concentration
EnergyATP from respiration
Proteinsspecific carrier proteins
Selectivitypump specific ions
Examples
  • Mineral ion uptake by root hair cells (soil has low ion concentration)
  • Glucose uptake from gut back into blood when concentration in gut is low
  • Sodium–potassium pump in nerve cells
Evidence it needs ATP Cyanide blocks respiration and halts active transport. Diffusion continues — proving the energy dependence.
Comparison table Diffusion: high→low, no energy. Osmosis: water, high→low water potential, no energy. Active: low→high, ATP required.
