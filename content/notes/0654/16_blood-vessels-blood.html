Three vessel types + four blood components. Structure fits function in every case.

Three vessels
Arterythick muscular wall · small lumen · no valves · away from heart · high pressure
Veinthin wall · wide lumen · valves · toward heart · low pressure
Capillaryone-cell-thick wall · tiny lumen · exchange happens here
Four blood components
  • Red blood cells (erythrocytes) — haemoglobin + O₂; biconcave; no nucleus
  • White blood cells — phagocytes (engulf pathogens), lymphocytes (antibodies)
  • Platelets — fragments that trigger clotting
  • Plasma — liquid carrying CO₂, glucose, hormones, urea
Haemoglobin In lungs (high O₂): Hb + 4O₂ → oxyhaemoglobin. In tissues (low O₂): oxyhaemoglobin → Hb + 4O₂.
Pulse You can feel a pulse in arteries (high pressure surges) but not veins. Capillaries are too small to feel.
