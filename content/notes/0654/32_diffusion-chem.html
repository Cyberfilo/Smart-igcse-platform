In chemistry, diffusion is particles spreading due to their kinetic energy. Fastest in gases, slowest in solids.

Rate ∝ temperature · Rate ∝ 1/√(molecular mass)
Evidence
  • Brown gas NO₂ spreading from one end of a container
  • Smell of perfume diffusing across a room
  • Coloured potassium permanganate crystal spreading in water
  • Brownian motion — smoke particles zigzag from collisions
Compare NH₃ and HCl In a glass tube with cotton wool soaked in NH₃ at one end and HCl at the other, white smoke forms closer to the HCl end — NH₃ (lighter) diffuses faster.
Particle KE Higher temperature = more kinetic energy → faster particle movement → faster diffusion. Always mention this in "explain" answers.
