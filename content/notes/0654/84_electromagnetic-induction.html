A changing magnetic field through a coil induces an EMF (voltage). Underpins generators, transformers, motors.

Faraday's demonstrations
  • Move magnet into coil → EMF induced (needle deflects)
  • Reverse direction → reversed EMF
  • Faster movement → bigger EMF
  • More turns on coil → bigger EMF
AC generator
A coil rotates in a magnetic field → induced EMF alternates → AC current.
Transformers (Extended)
Turns ratioV_s / V_p = N_s / N_p
PowerV_p × I_p = V_s × I_s (ideal)
  • Step-up transformer: more turns on secondary → higher V, lower I
  • Step-down: fewer turns on secondary
  • Electricity transmitted at high V to reduce I → less heat loss in cables
Example Transformer Np = 500, Ns = 100. Vp = 230 V → Vs = 230 × 100/500 = 46 V (step-down).
DC doesn't induce A constant DC current doesn't induce anything — only a CHANGING field creates EMF. That's why transformers only work with AC.
