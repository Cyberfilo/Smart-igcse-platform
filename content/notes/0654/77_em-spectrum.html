All EM waves travel at the speed of light (3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum). They differ in frequency (and wavelength).

The spectrum (long λ → short λ)
Radio · Microwaves · Infrared · Visible · Ultraviolet · X-rays · Gamma rays
Uses
Radiobroadcasting, radar
Microwavescooking, mobile phones, satellite
Infraredremote controls, thermal imaging, fibre optics
Visiblevision, photography
UVfluorescent lamps, security marking, sterilisation
X-raymedical imaging, airport scanners
Gammasterilising food/equipment, cancer treatment
Dangers
  • Microwaves: internal heating (eyes)
  • IR: skin burns
  • UV: skin cancer, eye damage
  • X-rays, gamma: ionising → cell damage, cancer
Same speed ALL EM waves travel at c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum. Only frequency (and therefore λ) differs. v = fλ still holds.
