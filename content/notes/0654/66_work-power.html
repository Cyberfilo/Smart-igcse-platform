Work = energy transferred by a force. Power = rate of doing work.

WorkW = F × d (joules)
PowerP = W / t (watts)
Power (from motion)P = F × v
Efficiencyuseful energy out / total energy in × 100%
Units
  • 1 J = 1 N × 1 m
  • 1 W = 1 J/s
  • 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (energy bill unit)
Example A crane lifts 500 kg through 20 m in 40 s. Work = mgh = 500 × 10 × 20 = 100 000 J. Power = 100 000 / 40 = 2500 W.
Efficiency Electric motor: 800 J in, 600 J useful (200 J heat). Efficiency = 600/800 × 100 = 75%.
Force must move If F acts but d = 0, no work done. Holding a book still does no work on it (mechanically, at least).
