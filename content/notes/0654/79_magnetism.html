Magnets exert non-contact forces on magnetic materials (iron, cobalt, nickel, steel) via invisible magnetic fields.

Magnetic rules
  • Like poles repel; unlike poles attract
  • Field lines go from N to S outside the magnet
  • Closer lines = stronger field
  • Field lines never cross
Soft vs hard magnetic materials
Soft ironmagnetises + demagnetises easily · electromagnet cores
Steelhard to magnetise, keeps magnetism · permanent magnets
Electromagnets
  • Current in a coil produces a magnetic field
  • Field strength increases with: more current, more turns, iron core
  • Switched on/off by controlling the current
  • Uses: cranes at scrap yards, relays, doorbells, MRI scanners
Right-hand grip rule Wrap right hand around a current-carrying wire with thumb in direction of current → fingers curl in direction of magnetic field.
Earth's field The Earth has a magnetic field — compass needles align with it. Geographic North is a magnetic SOUTH pole.
