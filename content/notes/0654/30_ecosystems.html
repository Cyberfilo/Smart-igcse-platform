Living things interact with each other + the environment. Energy flows one way; nutrients cycle.

Food chains
Producer → primary consumer → secondary consumer → tertiary consumer
Example grass → rabbit → fox. Grass is the producer; fox is the tertiary consumer.
Energy flow
  • Only ~10% of energy passes to each level; rest lost as heat (respiration) or not eaten
  • Explains why food chains rarely exceed 4–5 levels
  • Pyramids of numbers, biomass, and energy
Nutrient cycles
Carbon cyclephotosynthesis ↔ respiration · combustion · decomposition
Water cycleevaporation · condensation · precipitation · transpiration
Nitrogen cyclefixation · nitrification · uptake · decomposition
Human impact
  • Deforestation — less CO₂ uptake, biodiversity loss
  • Pollution — acid rain, eutrophication from fertilisers
  • Climate change — CO₂ + methane trap heat
  • Conservation — protected areas, sustainable forestry, captive breeding
