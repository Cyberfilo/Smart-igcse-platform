Respiration releases energy from glucose. Aerobic (with O₂) is the efficient default; anaerobic is emergency use.

Aerobic respiration
Wordglucose + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water (+ energy)
SymbolC₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O
  • Happens in mitochondria
  • Releases lots of energy (large ATP yield)
  • Continuous in nearly all cells
Anaerobic respiration
In muscleglucose → lactic acid (+ small amount of energy)
In yeastglucose → ethanol + CO₂ (+ small energy)
Oxygen debt
Lactic acid builds up in muscles → breathe heavily after exercise → extra O₂ oxidises lactic acid to CO₂ + H₂O.
Respiration ≠ breathing Breathing = ventilation, moving air in/out. Respiration = cellular process releasing energy. Different things — don't swap.
