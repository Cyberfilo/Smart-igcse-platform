Three ways heat moves: conduction (solids), convection (fluids), radiation (any; even vacuum).

Conduction
  • Particles vibrate and pass energy to neighbours
  • Metals excellent conductors (free electrons)
  • Non-metals + insulators poor conductors (air, wood, plastic)
Convection
  • Fluid (liquid/gas) warms → expands → less dense → rises
  • Cooler fluid sinks → convection current
  • Only in liquids and gases — not solids
  • Explains sea breezes, central heating, hot air balloons
Radiation
  • Infrared electromagnetic waves
  • Travels through vacuum (sunlight to Earth)
  • Dull black surfaces emit/absorb best
  • Shiny silvery surfaces emit/absorb worst (good reflectors)
