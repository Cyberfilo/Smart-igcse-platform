Gas exchange happens in alveoli — millions of tiny air sacs in the lungs, each ideally adapted for diffusion.

Alveolus adaptations
  • Many + small — huge total surface area
  • Thin wall (one cell thick) — short diffusion distance
  • Moist surface — gases dissolve for diffusion
  • Rich blood supply — maintains steep O₂/CO₂ gradients
Inhaled vs exhaled
Inhaled air21% O₂, 0.04% CO₂
Exhaled air16% O₂, 4% CO₂
Inhaledcooler, drier
Exhaledwarmer, more water vapour
Breathing mechanics
  • Inhale: intercostals pull ribs up + out; diaphragm flattens → volume ↑ → pressure ↓ → air in
  • Exhale: muscles relax; ribs drop; diaphragm domes → volume ↓ → pressure ↑ → air out
Smoking damage Cilia destroyed → mucus builds up (smoker's cough). Alveoli walls break down → emphysema → less SA for gas exchange.
