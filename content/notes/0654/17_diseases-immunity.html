Pathogens cause disease; the immune system fights back with phagocytes and antibodies.

Pathogens
  • Bacteria (cholera, TB), viruses (flu, HIV), fungi (athlete's foot), protoctists (malaria)
  • Spread via air, water, food, vectors, direct contact, bodily fluids
Body defences
Skinphysical barrier
Stomach acidkills ingested pathogens
Mucus + ciliatraps then sweeps away
Tearslysozyme enzyme
Immune response
  • Phagocytes engulf pathogens non-specifically
  • Lymphocytes produce antibodies specific to an antigen
  • Antibodies clump pathogens or mark them for destruction
  • Memory cells remain → rapid second response (immunity)
Vaccination
Inject weakened / dead pathogen → immune system makes antibodies + memory cells → future infection triggers fast response.
Active vs passive Active immunity: body makes own antibodies (longer lasting). Passive: receive ready-made antibodies (fast, short — e.g. breast milk).
