Giant covalent structures have countless atoms linked by covalent bonds. Metals are giant lattices with a "sea of electrons".

Giant covalent
Diamond
  • Each C bonded to 4 others (tetrahedral)
  • Hardest natural substance
  • Very high m.p.
  • Does NOT conduct (no free electrons)
Graphite
  • Each C bonded to 3 others in layers
  • Layers slip → soft, slippery
  • 1 free e⁻ per C → conducts
  • Lubricant + electrodes
Metallic bonding
Regular lattice of positive metal ions · delocalised ("sea") electrons move freely
Properties explained
  • Conducts electricity + heat — free electrons carry charge/energy
  • Malleable and ductile — layers of ions can slide past each other
  • High m.p. — strong electrostatic attraction between ions and electrons
  • Shiny — electrons reflect light
Silicon dioxide SiO₂ (quartz) is also a giant covalent structure — high m.p., hard, non-conductor. Similar to diamond.
