Renewable resources replenish naturally; non-renewable will run out. Each has pros and cons.

Non-renewable
  • Coal, oil, gas (fossil fuels) — reliable, high energy density, but emit CO₂ and SO₂
  • Nuclear — fission of U-235. High energy density, no CO₂, but radioactive waste + decommissioning cost
Renewable
  • Solar — PV cells / solar heating · no emissions · weather + night dependent
  • Wind — turbines · no emissions · intermittent, needs open land
  • Hydroelectric — flowing water · reliable · changes ecosystems
  • Geothermal — heat from Earth's interior · steady · location-limited
  • Biomass — burning plant/animal matter · ~carbon-neutral · takes land
  • Tidal / wave — predictable, no emissions · expensive infrastructure
Sun is the ultimate source
Most resources (solar, wind, wave, biomass, fossil fuels) trace back to the Sun. Exceptions: geothermal, nuclear, tidal (Moon).
