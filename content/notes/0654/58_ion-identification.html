Confirm unknown ions with specific tests. Memorise colours and gases.

Cation tests — flame + NaOH
Flame testLi red · Na yellow · K lilac · Ca orange-red · Cu blue-green
+ NaOHCu²⁺ blue ppt · Fe²⁺ green · Fe³⁺ brown · Zn²⁺ white (dissolves in excess) · NH₄⁺ → ammonia gas on warming
Anion tests
  • Carbonate (CO₃²⁻): + acid → CO₂ gas (limewater turns milky)
  • Chloride (Cl⁻): + AgNO₃ → white ppt (dissolves in NH₃)
  • Bromide: + AgNO₃ → cream ppt
  • Iodide: + AgNO₃ → yellow ppt
  • Sulfate (SO₄²⁻): + BaCl₂ → white ppt
  • Nitrate: + NaOH + Al → ammonia gas (on warming)
Gas tests
  • O₂ — relights a glowing splint
  • H₂ — squeaky pop with lit splint
  • CO₂ — turns limewater milky
  • Cl₂ — bleaches damp litmus paper
  • NH₃ — turns damp red litmus blue
Silver nitrate specifics Always acidify with dilute HNO₃ first (removes interfering ions like CO₃²⁻ that would also precipitate).
