Rusting destroys iron; extraction methods depend on the metal's reactivity.

Rusting of iron
iron + oxygen + water → hydrated iron(III) oxide (rust)
  • Both O₂ AND H₂O needed (experiment: iron in dry air doesn't rust, iron in boiled water sealed off doesn't rust)
  • Prevention: paint, oil, galvanising (Zn coat), plastic coat, sacrificial protection (Mg/Zn blocks)
Sacrificial protection
Attach a more reactive metal (Zn, Mg). It corrodes instead of the iron. Used on ships' hulls + underground pipelines.

Blast furnace (iron) Iron ore + coke + limestone. C + O₂ → CO₂; CO₂ + C → 2CO; Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂. Slag (CaSiO₃) floats on molten iron.
Why Al by electrolysis? Al is too reactive to reduce with C. Bauxite (Al₂O₃) dissolved in molten cryolite, then electrolysed. Energy-intensive → expensive.
