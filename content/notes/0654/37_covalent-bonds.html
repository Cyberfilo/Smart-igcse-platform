Two non-metals form bonds by sharing electrons. Each shared pair is one covalent bond.

Simple molecules
  • H₂ — single bond (1 pair)
  • O₂ — double bond (2 pairs)
  • N₂ — triple bond (3 pairs)
  • H₂O — O has 2 lone pairs + 2 bonds
  • CH₄ — C has 4 single bonds to H
  • NH₃ — N has 1 lone pair + 3 bonds

Dot-and-cross (H₂O) O has 6 outer electrons. Shares 1 with each H. Each bond shown as one dot + one cross. O then has 2 lone pairs (2 dots each).
Strong covalent bond, weak IMF The bond WITHIN a molecule (covalent) is strong. The forces BETWEEN molecules (intermolecular) are weak — that's why m.p. is low.
