Centre of gravity = single point where the whole weight of an object appears to act. Position determines stability.

Regular vs irregular
  • Uniform shape (rectangle, circle): CoG at geometric centre
  • Irregular shape: find using a plumb line — hang from one point, mark vertical; repeat from another; intersection = CoG
Stability
An object topples when the vertical line through its CoG falls outside the base.
Stablelow CoG, wide base (racing car)
Unstablehigh CoG, narrow base (broom on finger)
NeutralCoG stays at same height if rotated (ball on flat)
Example A double-decker bus: heavy engine at the bottom lowers CoG → more stable around corners. Tall passengers on top raise CoG → less stable.
CoG outside object Can be outside the object (e.g. ring, boomerang). The centre of gravity is a point, not necessarily material.
