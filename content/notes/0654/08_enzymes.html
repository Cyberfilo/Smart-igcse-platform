Enzymes are biological catalysts — proteins that speed up reactions. Each has a specific 3D active site (lock-and-key model).

Lock and keySubstrate fits the active site → enzyme-substrate complex → products released
Factors affecting rate
  • Temperature — optimum near 37 °C in humans; too high denatures the enzyme
  • pH — optimum varies (pepsin pH 2, amylase pH 7); extremes denature
  • Substrate concentration — rate ↑ until enzymes are saturated
  • Enzyme concentration — more enzymes → faster (until substrate runs out)
Denaturation
High temp / extreme pH breaks bonds holding the 3D shape. Active site changes → substrate no longer fits. Irreversible.
Example Catalase in liver decomposes H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂. Boiled liver produces no bubbles — enzyme denatured.
"Denatured" vs "killed" Enzymes are molecules, not alive. They denature, not die. Use the right word.
