Two transport tissues: xylem (water up) and phloem (sugars anywhere).

Xylem
  • Hollow dead cells, lignin-reinforced walls
  • Transports water + minerals
  • One direction: roots → leaves
  • Driven by transpiration pull
Phloem
  • Living sieve tube elements + companion cells
  • Transports dissolved sucrose + amino acids
  • Both directions (translocation)
  • Active process, uses ATP
Transpiration
Loss of water vapour from leaves (mainly through stomata). Creates the pull that draws water up the xylem.
  • Rate increases with: temperature, wind, dry air, light intensity
  • Rate measured with a potometer
Potometer Measure distance an air bubble moves in a capillary tube over time. Larger movement = faster transpiration.
